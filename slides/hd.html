Arduino's inputs en outputs

  • Digital inputs (pins 0-13) measure thruth values;
    (true/false, HIGH/LOW). 0V = false, +5V = true
  • Analog inputs (pins A0-A5) measure between 0 nad 1024, van 0V tot +5V.
  • Digital outputs (pins 0-13) have +5V as "true" (HIGH), 0V as "false" (LOW)
  • Analog outputs not possible directly; arduino (pins 3, 5, 6, 9, 10, 11) uses "pulse width modulation" as simulation.

Circuit hints

  • It's just three wires!
  • Wire w/o resistor must go in +5V
  • Wire w/ resistor soldered to it goes in A0
  • Other end of resistor goes in GND

(You're building a voltage divider because the arduino can't sense resistance, only voltage)
